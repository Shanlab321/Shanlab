Research Focus

Computational Materials Discovery through DFT and MD

Overview

My research is rooted in the computational modeling of advanced materials, with a focus on Density Functional Theory (DFT) and Molecular Dynamics (MD) simulations. I use tools such as CASTEP, DMol³, and Forcite to explore the electronic, optical, thermal, and adsorptive behavior of functional materials. My long-term vision is to bridge the gap between theoretical predictions and experimental realities, while empowering students and early-career researchers through SHAN Academy.

Current Research Areas

  • Electronic and Optical Properties of Oxides: Studying band structures, DOS, and absorption spectra of oxide materials using CASTEP to identify candidates for photocatalysis and optoelectronics.
  • Defect Engineering in Semiconductors: Exploring the effects of mono-, co-, and tri-doping on TiO₂ and similar systems to tailor their electronic and magnetic properties.
  • Molecular Dynamics of Nanocomposites: Simulating atomic dynamics and thermal properties of composite materials using Forcite and GULP to assess stability and performance.
  • Surface and Adsorption Studies: Modeling the reactivity of various TiO₂ and CeO₂ surface terminations (e.g., (101), (001), (111)) and analyzing molecule-surface interactions using DMol³ and Adsorption Locator.
  • DFT-Based Material Design for Sensors: Designing and evaluating materials for gas sensing applications by simulating charge transfer and adsorption energies.
  • Heterojunctions and Nanostructures: Working on CeO₂/CoFe₂O₄ and ZnO/Fe₂O₃ heterojunctions to understand interfacial behavior, charge mobility, and catalytic potential.
  • Nano Clusters and Perovskite Materials: Investigating the properties of transition metal nano clusters and perovskite systems for their optical, electronic, and energy-related applications.
  • DFT+U Enhanced by Machine Learning: Currently integrating machine learning models to predict U parameters for DFT+U simulations, enhancing material property predictions and aligning them with experimental outcomes.

Tools and Techniques

  • DFT Calculations: CASTEP, DMol³, Quantum ESPRESSO
  • MD Simulations: Forcite, GULP
  • Advanced Analysis: Band structure, DOS, PDOS, phonon spectra, IR
  • Surface & Adsorption Modeling: Adsorption Locator, surface slab construction
  • Charge Studies: Mulliken, Hirshfeld, ELF, charge density differences
  • Visualization: VESTA, Origin, MATLAB

Research Goals

  • Predict and design efficient materials for photocatalysis, gas sensing, and energy harvesting
  • Integrate AI and machine learning into computational workflows for materials discovery
  • Support experimental work with accurate, theory-backed insights
  • Build a strong training platform through SHAN Academy to guide young researchers in mastering DFT and MD simulation tools

Collaborations and Student Engagement

I actively welcome research collaboration and student involvement. Through SHAN Academy, I mentor students interested in quantum simulations and materials science, helping them build practical skills in a virtual lab environment.
